Division (None - too late to enter a league)

Cloonfad United was the vision of John O'Malley and Paddy Regan (the "godfathers" of Cloonfad soccer), and a lot of hard work were needed behinds the scenes by the newly formed committee before any ball was kicked. When all the i's were dotted and the t's crossed, Cloonfad United could now concentrate on the task at hand, their inclusion in the Roscommon and League Cups. Pre-Cup "friendlies" were organised and played to give the new up and coming team the best chance for success. Cloonfad United were stepping out into the unknown....

1979/1980 Roscommon Cup

And so it began.......after a lot of friendly's and change of managers???, Cloonfad United first taste of competitive football finally arrived. On the 04th Feb 1979 Cloonfad's first game was in the Roscommon Challenge cup against Tarmon Rovers. A convoy of nine cars left Cloonfad that bright Sunday afternoon bringing the players to the game, the supporters would follow on later. Amazing Cloonfad led three times in their first Roscommon Cup match only to draw 4-4, the reports state that Tarmon was not the cleanest of teams!!! There was however at the very least another day out for Cloonfad in the replay two weeks later and the chance to play in their brand new stadium (John Pa Burkes field). This did not to go plan when Cloonfad went down 3-1 and out of the Roscommon Cup.

1979/1980 League Cup

Cloonfad did enter the then League Cup (Divisional Cup for each league now), some records stated otherwise and were draw into Section D with Ballymoe & Ballintubber both who were in Divsion 2 at the time. The first outing Cloonfad recorded their first every victory in RDFL football when they beat Ballymoe Rangers 2-1 in Cloonfad. Unfortunately they lost the second and most important game to qualify for the semi-final when they were narrowly beaten 4-3 in Ballintubber.

1979/1980 Manager

Various Managers

Paddy Regan













John Raferty

 Cloonfad United had some Managerial issues with their pre-cup friendlies. On the inaugural meeting on the 17th Oct Johnny Moran was voted into the position of Manager from a list containing, F Green, S Brennan JP Burke, Johnny Moran, John Keane, Paddy Regan and Nessan Burke. The last 3 in as player managers but were opposed.
By Nov 20th Johnny Moran had resigned from the postion. Paddy Regan was voted to be the next manager by 4 votes to 3 over Nessan Burke and held the position of Manager for Cloonfad official RDFL Roscommon Challenage Cup matches.

The minutes of the meeting held on the 29th Mar 1979 stated that Paddy Regan peferred not to be manger and stood down. John Finnegan nonimated John Raftery as Manager and himself as assistant. They were voted in and John Raftery became the official manager for the league Cup matches in May.
