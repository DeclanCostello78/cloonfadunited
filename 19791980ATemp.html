( Cloonfad United's 1st official Season )


"The 1979-1980 Season could not come quickly enough for a Cloonfad team which longed to test its football skills against the other ten sides in Division 2. They now had a settled panel and on the village outskirts one of John Pa Burke’s fields for their stadium. They had their new yellow and blue strip — designed by team member Brian Martyn — and an ambitious player-manager in Sean Corcoran." – 21st Anniversary Magazine.

1979/1980 Divsion 2 League

With it been Cloonfad's first venture into the league it's hard to judge their overall performance. Cloonfad finished eighth of 11 teams, playing a total of 20 games that season. A good bench mark would be the fact that they finished above teams that had played the previous season. If Cloonfad had charged at the gate it would have been value for money as most of the 20 games had goals goals goals, 138 in fact. Only one game did Cloonfad not score, the 1-0 defeat to CP Ajax.
Cloonfad biggest league win that season was an impressive 8 - 5 away to Lough Harps (seriously 8-5!!!!!!!).

But this did not sway Cloonfad and finally their attack paid off when Brian Martyn broke through and crossed a ball and John Mullarkey put it in the net with his head. Cloonfad kept on the pressure and came very close many times. But Ballinasloe town scored again and made it 4:1 In the last five minutes John Keane got one more back for Cloonfad." - Club Secretary.

1979/1980 Manager

Sean Corcoran — assisted by John Keane

"And so it had come and went, Cloonfad's first season as an emerging football club was complete. Sean Corcoran first season as Player/Manager didn't yield silverware or promotion but it did gain him valuable experience. A total of 23 games were played and Cloonfad would win just over a quarter of those games. An incredible 91 goals were conceded that first season which would explain the 15 defeats from 23. However the club would score 61 goals from those 23 games which gave Sean hope for the future."








1979/1980 Top Scorer

John Keane

"John Keane will always have the honor of becoming Cloonfad United's first every official Season top-scorer when he bagged 17 goals in their first season, three coming from the penalty spot. He did however score 5 more goals in all competitions but alas these will not be counted as they were not in official RDFL games. Sorry John...... "








Points of Intrest



  • Cloonfad's first every league victory was away to neighbours Lough Harps.
  • Funny enough Cloonfad's first home league victory was also against our neighbours Lough Harps.
  • Nessan Burke would have the honor of captained the team their first season.
  • Cloonfad conceeded a record 91 goals their first year. A record thankfully not beaten to this day.
  • Cloonfad defeated rivals Lough Harps in both games in league to start an unbeaten run against their neighbours that would last 16 years.
